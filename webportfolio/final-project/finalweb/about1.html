About Us

The Woman Who Listened When the Echoes Called

The Eternal Echoes Society was born from the life’s work of its founder, Dr. Liora Vey, a spiritual anthropologist and intuitive researcher whose lifelong fascination with the soul’s journey began in childhood. As a young girl, Liora experienced vivid dreams of places she had never visited and lives she had never lived — memories that felt more real than imagination. These early visions led her on a path of deep study into reincarnation, ancestral memory, and spiritual traditions from around the world, blending academic rigor with an openness to the unseen.

While traveling through remote regions of Tibet, Egypt, and the Celtic isles, Liora encountered mystics, shamans, and seers who spoke of souls that linger — those who wander between worlds, bound by sorrow, duty, or confusion. One night, during a meditation in an abandoned monastery, she received what she described as a “soul call” — a direct encounter with a presence that was neither threatening nor lost, but deeply weary. This moment crystallized her calling: not just to study these echoes of consciousness, but to help them find peace. From this experience, the vision for the Eternal Echoes Society emerged.

Founded in 2009, the Society began as a small circle of researchers, mediums, and spiritual healers dedicated to mapping the unseen layers of the soul’s existence. What started as a series of quiet investigations and spiritual sessions grew into a global initiative — offering guidance, conducting soul-resonance research, and helping individuals connect with the echoes that surround them. Today, under Liora’s quiet but luminous leadership, the Eternal Echoes Society continues to honor the sacred mystery of the soul’s path, offering compassion and understanding to those still finding their way through the in-between.
